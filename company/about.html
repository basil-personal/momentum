About Momentum

We are building the ambient layer between you and your work — technology so intuitive it disappears into your day.

Our Mission

We believe the best technology is the kind you forget is there. Momentum exists to remove the friction between intention and action — to give professionals back the headspace they lose to context-switching, note-taking, and the endless administrative overhead of modern knowledge work.

Every product decision we make is measured against a single question: does this help someone stay in flow? If the answer is no, we don't build it.

How It Started

In early 2024, co-founders Elara Voss and Marcus Chen were running a fast-growing product studio. They sat in eight to twelve meetings a day, and every single one started the same way: five minutes of scrambling to recall what was decided last time, who owned which action item, and what had changed since.

They tried every note-taking app, every AI transcription tool, every shared doc workflow. Nothing stuck — because every solution still required someone to actively operate it. The real problem wasn't the software. It was the assumption that humans should be the ones capturing context in the first place.

So they built a small wearable prototype that could listen, understand, and organize meeting context without anyone pressing a button. Friends wanted one. Then their friends wanted one. Within three months they had a waitlist of four thousand people and a conviction that ambient computing wasn't a gimmick — it was the next paradigm.

Momentum was incorporated in New York City in June 2024. The rest has been an exercise in moving quickly and quietly — exactly the way we think technology should work.

Our Values

Calm by Design

We reject the attention economy. Our products never buzz, ping, or interrupt unless explicitly asked. Calmness is not a feature — it is the architecture. Every interaction is designed to lower cognitive load, not raise it.

Privacy as Architecture

Privacy at Momentum is not a toggle in settings — it is baked into silicon. Audio is processed on-device, transcripts are encrypted end-to-end, and we have zero access to your data. We chose the harder engineering path so our users never have to choose between utility and trust.

Hardware That Breathes

Technology should feel like an extension of the body, not a burden on it. We obsess over weight, texture, thermal comfort, and wearability. If a device makes you conscious of wearing it, we go back to the drawing board.
