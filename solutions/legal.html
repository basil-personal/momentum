In legal work, what was said — and how it was said — is everything.

Attorneys live and die by the details. A client mentions a date, a name, a sequence of events during an intake meeting — and the accuracy of that record can determine the outcome of an entire case. Yet most law firms still rely on handwritten notes, associate memory, and post-meeting dictation to document their most critical conversations.

The cost of imprecision is staggering. Missed details lead to follow-up calls that bill client time unnecessarily. Inconsistencies between an attorney's notes and the client's recollection create credibility issues. And the sheer volume of meetings at a busy firm means that even the most diligent note-taker will miss things that matter.

Legal professionals need a recording solution that's accurate enough to be relied upon, secure enough to protect privilege, and unobtrusive enough that it doesn't change the dynamic of a client conversation.

Precision tools for the precision profession

Client Meeting Documentation

Every client meeting produces a complete, searchable transcript with speaker identification and timestamps. Attorneys can review exactly what was discussed, search for specific terms or dates, and generate meeting summaries that become part of the case file.

Deposition Support

Use Momentum as a secondary recording during depositions and witness interviews. The high-fidelity audio capture and accurate transcription provide a reliable backup record that attorneys can search and annotate during case preparation.

Privilege-Aware Data Handling

Momentum's security architecture is designed with attorney-client privilege in mind. Granular access controls, encryption at rest and in transit, and configurable retention policies ensure that privileged communications receive the protection they require.

Accurate Transcription

Legal work demands accuracy that consumer-grade transcription cannot deliver. Momentum's transcription engine is optimized for the vocabulary, cadence, and complexity of legal conversations — capturing names, statutes, case citations, and technical terminology with reliable precision.
