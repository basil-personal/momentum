Clinicians spend more time documenting care than delivering it.

The average physician spends two hours on documentation for every one hour of patient care. They stay late clicking through EHR fields, dictating notes from memory, and copying information between systems. The result is burnout, reduced patient face time, and documentation that's often incomplete because it was reconstructed hours after the encounter.

Scribes help but don't scale. Ambient dictation tools have improved but still require clinicians to narrate their thinking rather than simply practice medicine. What healthcare needs is a device that sits quietly in the exam room, captures the entire encounter, and produces documentation that flows directly into existing clinical systems — with the security and compliance guarantees that healthcare demands.

Healthcare-grade recording, built for clinical workflows

Patient Encounter Documentation

Momentum captures the natural conversation between clinician and patient, then generates structured clinical notes — including chief complaint, history of present illness, assessment, and plan. Clinicians review and approve rather than create from scratch.

HIPAA Compliance

Momentum is built to meet the stringent requirements of healthcare data protection. End-to-end encryption, BAA agreements, configurable retention policies, and audit logging ensure that protected health information is handled with the highest standard of care.

EHR Integration

Generated documentation flows directly into your electronic health record system. Momentum integrates with major EHR platforms so that clinical notes, encounter summaries, and relevant data populate the right fields without manual entry or copy-pasting.

Hands-Free Operation

The Momentum device requires no interaction during the encounter. Place it in the exam room and it handles everything — recording, transcription, and note generation. Clinicians never touch a button, open a laptop, or break eye contact with their patient.

A primary care clinic that gave its doctors two hours back every day

A six-physician primary care practice in the Midwest was losing clinicians to burnout. The average doctor was spending three to four hours each evening finishing documentation — time taken from family, rest, and the energy needed to show up fully for patients the next day.

The practice placed Momentum devices in each of their twelve exam rooms. After a two-week pilot with two physicians, the results were immediate: documentation time dropped by over sixty percent. Physicians reviewed and approved AI-generated notes in minutes rather than constructing them from memory over hours.

Patient satisfaction scores improved because clinicians made more eye contact and asked more follow-up questions. One physician reported that for the first time in years, she left the office at five o'clock — and the notes were already done.

Within a month, all six physicians had adopted the system. The practice estimated annual savings equivalent to a full-time scribe salary, with higher documentation accuracy and zero disruption to patient flow.
