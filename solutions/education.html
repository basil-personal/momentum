Designed for the rhythms of academic life

Lecture Recording

Place a Momentum Conference device in any classroom or lecture hall. It captures every session automatically — no setup, no start button, no IT support ticket. Faculty can focus on teaching while students get searchable, time-stamped transcripts of every class.

Research Interview Capture

Graduate students and faculty conducting qualitative research can use Momentum Personal to capture interviews with broadcast-quality audio. Transcripts are generated automatically, saving dozens of hours of manual transcription per project.

Accessibility Compliance

Momentum generates accurate transcripts for every recorded session, helping institutions meet ADA and Section 508 requirements. Students who need captions, transcripts, or the ability to review material at their own pace get access automatically.

Student Privacy

Momentum is designed with FERPA compliance in mind. Configurable access controls ensure that recordings are only available to authorized individuals. Students can be confident that their classroom participation is handled with the same care as any other educational record.

A university department that made every seminar accessible

The sociology department at a mid-size research university installed Momentum Conference devices in their four seminar rooms and two faculty meeting spaces. The goal was simple: make every academic conversation available to anyone who needed it.

Within the first semester, students reported a measurable improvement in their ability to review complex material. International students who previously struggled with the pace of English-language seminars could revisit transcripts at their own speed. Students with documented disabilities no longer had to request accommodations separately — the transcripts were simply there.

Faculty researchers began using the devices to capture guest speaker visits and thesis defenses. One professor used Momentum Personal to record forty field interviews for a longitudinal study, eliminating three months of transcription work and allowing her team to begin analysis immediately.

"For the first time, our seminar discussions aren't ephemeral. Students can revisit a guest lecture from three weeks ago, search for the exact moment a concept was explained, and engage with the material on a deeper level. And for our researchers, the time saved on transcription alone has been transformative."
