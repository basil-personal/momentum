Your best insights happen when you're listening, not writing.

Consulting is a listening profession. The most valuable thing you can do in a client meeting is be fully present — hearing not just what's being said, but what's behind it. The organizational dynamics, the unspoken concerns, the offhand comment that reveals the real problem.

But the moment you start taking notes, you fracture your attention. You're choosing which details to capture and which to let go. You're looking down at your laptop instead of reading the room. And when you sit down later to write the deliverable, you're working from fragments rather than the full picture.

Consultants need a way to be completely present in client conversations while knowing that every word, every nuance, and every detail is being preserved for when they need it.
