Accessibility Statement

Last updated: March 1, 2026

1. Our Commitment to Accessibility

At Momentum, we believe that technology should be usable by everyone. We are committed to ensuring that our products and services — including our wearable device, companion mobile applications, and website — are accessible to people with disabilities. Accessibility is not an afterthought at Momentum; it is a core value that informs our design and engineering decisions from the earliest stages of product development.

We strive to meet or exceed the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards across all of our digital experiences, and we are continually working to improve the accessibility of our hardware and software products. Our goal is to ensure that every person, regardless of ability, can benefit from Momentum's AI-powered transcription and meeting intelligence features.

2. WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance Status

Momentum is committed to conforming to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards across our website, web dashboard, and companion mobile applications. We regularly evaluate our digital properties against these standards and work to address any areas of non-conformance. Our current compliance status is as follows:

  • Website (momentum.so): Substantially conforms to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Our website has been audited by an independent accessibility consultant, and we are actively addressing any identified issues.
  • Web Dashboard: Substantially conforms to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. All core functionality, including transcription viewing, search, account management, and settings, is accessible via keyboard navigation and compatible with screen readers.
  • iOS App: Substantially conforms to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. The app is fully compatible with VoiceOver and supports Dynamic Type for system-level font size preferences.
  • Android App: Substantially conforms to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. The app is fully compatible with TalkBack and supports system-level font size and display size preferences.

"Substantially conforms" means that while we have made significant efforts to ensure conformance, there may be some areas that are not yet fully compliant. We are committed to addressing these areas and continue to make improvements on an ongoing basis.

3. Accessible Features of Momentum Hardware

The Momentum wearable device has been designed with accessibility in mind from its inception. Key accessibility features of the hardware include:

3.1 Tactile Interaction Design

The Momentum device features a carefully designed tactile interface that allows users to operate the device entirely by touch, without the need to look at a screen. The device includes a textured surface with distinct tactile zones for different functions, a raised orientation indicator so you can always tell which way the device is facing, a physical button with clear tactile feedback for primary actions (start/stop recording), and haptic vibration feedback that confirms actions and communicates device status. All gestures and interactions have been designed to be simple and discoverable, with consistent haptic feedback patterns that can be learned quickly.

3.2 Audio Feedback

The Momentum device provides audio cues for important events and status changes, including a distinct tone when recording starts and stops, audio confirmation of successful Bluetooth pairing and connection, spoken battery level announcements when the device is picked up or placed on the charger, and configurable audio alerts for low battery, storage capacity, and firmware updates. Audio feedback volume is adjustable through the companion app, and users can enable a mode that provides spoken descriptions of all device actions and status changes.

3.3 LED Indicators

The device includes multi-color LED indicators that convey device status at a glance. For users who are color-blind, the LEDs use distinct blinking patterns in addition to colors to differentiate between states. The LED states include a solid green light for actively recording, a pulsing blue light for Bluetooth pairing mode, a solid amber light for charging, a solid green light for fully charged, and a flashing red light for low battery or error conditions. LED brightness is adjustable to accommodate users with light sensitivity, and LEDs can be fully disabled for users who prefer to rely on audio and haptic feedback alone.

4. App Accessibility Features

4.1 Screen Reader Compatibility

The Momentum companion app is fully compatible with platform screen readers. On iOS, the app has been built with comprehensive VoiceOver support. All interactive elements have descriptive accessibility labels, images include alternative text descriptions, custom controls are properly annotated with accessibility traits and hints, and the app follows a logical focus order that matches the visual layout. On Android, the app is fully compatible with TalkBack. All views and controls have appropriate content descriptions, and the app supports Explore by Touch for intuitive navigation.

4.2 High Contrast and Visual Accessibility

The Momentum app supports several visual accessibility features. All text and interactive elements meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA contrast ratio requirements (minimum 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text). The app supports both a standard light theme and a high-contrast dark theme that has been specifically designed to maximize readability for users with low vision. The app also supports the system-level high-contrast and bold text settings on both iOS and Android, adjusting its appearance automatically when these settings are enabled.

4.3 Dynamic Type and Text Scaling

The Momentum app fully supports Dynamic Type on iOS and system font scaling on Android. All text in the app, including transcription content, navigation elements, and settings, will scale according to your system-level font size preferences. The app has been tested and optimized for all supported font sizes, including the largest accessibility sizes, to ensure that layouts remain functional and readable. Users can also adjust the font size for transcription content independently within the app's settings.

4.4 Keyboard and Switch Access

The Momentum web dashboard and website are fully navigable using a keyboard alone. All interactive elements are reachable via Tab key navigation, and the current focus is always visually indicated with a clear, high-contrast focus ring. The web dashboard supports standard keyboard shortcuts for common actions, and a keyboard shortcut reference is available by pressing the "?" key. The mobile app supports external keyboard navigation on both iOS and Android, as well as Switch Access on Android and Switch Control on iOS.

4.5 Motion and Animation

The Momentum app respects the "Reduce Motion" setting on iOS and the "Remove Animations" setting on Android. When these settings are enabled, the app disables non-essential animations and transitions to provide a more comfortable experience for users who are sensitive to motion. On the web, we honor the "prefers-reduced-motion" CSS media query.

5. Web Accessibility

The Momentum website and web dashboard have been built with accessibility as a priority. Key web accessibility features include semantic HTML markup with appropriate use of headings, landmarks, lists, and ARIA attributes; a skip navigation link that allows keyboard users to jump directly to the main content; consistent and predictable navigation across all pages; form inputs with associated labels, error messages, and instructions; tables with proper headers and captions for data presentation; sufficient color contrast throughout the site (minimum 4.5:1 for text); responsive design that works across devices and supports zoom up to 400% without loss of content or functionality; and all media content with text alternatives, captions, or transcripts as appropriate.

6. Known Limitations

While we strive for comprehensive accessibility, we are aware of the following limitations in our current products:

  • Real-time transcription display: The live transcription view in the mobile app may not always announce new content immediately to screen readers during active recording. We are working on improving the live region behavior to provide more timely updates.
  • Third-party integrations: Some third-party services that integrate with Momentum (such as embedded calendar views or CRM widgets) may not fully conform to WCAG 2.1 AA standards. We work with our integration partners to improve accessibility and provide alternative workflows where possible.
  • Legacy PDF exports: Some older PDF export templates may not be fully tagged for screen reader accessibility. We have updated our default export templates to be accessible, but older exports generated before this update may have limited accessibility.
  • Complex data visualizations: Some analytics charts in the web dashboard may not be fully accessible to screen reader users. We provide text-based data tables as alternatives for all chart visualizations.

We are actively working to address each of these limitations and welcome feedback from our users to help prioritize improvements.

7. Testing and Evaluation

We employ a multi-layered approach to accessibility testing:

  • Automated testing: We use automated accessibility testing tools (including axe-core and Lighthouse) as part of our continuous integration pipeline to catch common accessibility issues before code reaches production.
  • Manual testing: Our QA team conducts regular manual accessibility testing using screen readers (VoiceOver, TalkBack, NVDA, JAWS), keyboard-only navigation, and high-contrast display settings.
  • External audits: We engage independent accessibility consultants to conduct comprehensive WCAG 2.1 audits of our digital properties at least annually.
  • User testing: We work with users who have disabilities to conduct usability testing and gather feedback on our products. Their insights are invaluable in shaping our accessibility improvements.

8. Feedback and Contact Information

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of Momentum products and services. If you encounter an accessibility barrier, have suggestions for improvement, or need assistance using any of our products, please contact us. We take accessibility feedback seriously and will work to address reported issues promptly.

You can reach our accessibility team through the following channels:

Email: accessibility@momentum.so
Response time: We aim to acknowledge accessibility feedback within 2 business days and provide a substantive response within 10 business days.

When contacting us about an accessibility issue, it is helpful to include a description of the issue you encountered, the product and platform you were using (e.g., iOS app, web dashboard, website), the assistive technology you were using (e.g., VoiceOver, TalkBack, NVDA, keyboard-only), and the steps to reproduce the issue. This information helps us investigate and resolve issues more effectively.

9. Continuous Improvement

Accessibility is an ongoing effort, not a destination. We are committed to continually improving the accessibility of our products and services. Our accessibility roadmap includes regular WCAG conformance audits and remediation, accessibility training for all product and engineering team members, incorporating accessibility requirements into our design system and component library, engaging with disability advocacy organizations and communities, and staying current with evolving accessibility standards and best practices, including preparing for WCAG 2.2 and WCAG 3.0 as they are finalized.

We believe that building accessible products makes them better for everyone, and we are committed to this work for the long term.
