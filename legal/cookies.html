1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to provide a better browsing experience, and to supply information to the site owners. Cookies may be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third-party services that the website uses ("third-party cookies"). Cookies can remain on your device for different periods of time: "session cookies" exist only while your browser is open and are deleted automatically once you close your browser, while "persistent cookies" survive after your browser is closed and can be used to recognize your device when you return to the website.

In addition to cookies, we may use similar technologies such as web beacons (also known as pixel tags or clear GIFs), local storage, and device fingerprinting to collect and store information. References to "cookies" in this policy include these similar technologies unless otherwise noted.

2. How We Use Cookies

Momentum uses cookies and similar technologies on our website (momentum.so) and web dashboard for several purposes. We use them to ensure our website functions properly and securely, to remember your preferences and settings, to understand how visitors interact with our website, and to improve our website and services based on usage patterns. We do not use cookies for advertising or to track your activity across other websites. Momentum does not serve targeted advertisements and does not allow third-party advertising networks to place cookies on our website.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of our website and web dashboard. They enable core functionality such as security, user authentication, session management, and accessibility features. Without these cookies, the website cannot function properly, and they cannot be disabled. Essential cookies do not collect personal information for marketing purposes.

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. We use analytics cookies to measure the number of visitors, identify which pages are visited most frequently, understand how visitors navigate through the site, identify technical issues and areas for improvement, and measure the effectiveness of our content. We use Mixpanel as our analytics provider. All analytics data is aggregated and does not personally identify individual visitors. You may opt out of analytics cookies at any time using the cookie preferences panel on our website.

3.3 Preference Cookies

These cookies allow our website to remember choices you have made in the past, such as your preferred language, your display name, your theme preference (light or dark mode), your region or timezone, and whether you have dismissed certain notifications or banners. These cookies make your experience more personalized and convenient. If you disable preference cookies, some customization features may not function properly.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We use a limited number of third-party services that may set their own cookies on your device when you visit our website. These third-party cookies are subject to the respective third parties' privacy policies, not this Cookie Policy. The third-party services we use include:

  • Mixpanel — for website analytics and usage measurement. Mixpanel's privacy policy is available at mixpanel.com/legal/privacy-policy.
  • Stripe — for payment processing on our checkout pages. Stripe may set cookies to prevent fraud and process payments securely. Stripe's privacy policy is available at stripe.com/privacy.
  • Intercom — for our customer support chat widget. Intercom may set cookies to maintain your support conversation across page visits. Intercom's privacy policy is available at intercom.com/legal/privacy.

We regularly review the third-party services we use and their cookie practices to ensure they align with our privacy standards. We do not allow any third-party advertising cookies on our website.

6. How to Manage Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies:

6.1 Cookie Preferences Panel

When you first visit our website, a cookie consent banner will appear allowing you to accept or customize your cookie preferences. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Preferences" link in the footer of any page on our website. Note that essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are required for the basic functionality of the website.

6.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically configure your browser to block all cookies, block third-party cookies only, delete cookies when you close your browser, or be notified when a website sets a cookie. The process for managing cookies varies by browser. Please refer to the help documentation for your specific browser:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

Please be aware that blocking or deleting certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. If you block essential cookies, you may not be able to log in to your account or use certain features of the web dashboard.

6.3 Opt-Out of Analytics

If you wish to opt out of analytics tracking specifically, you may do so through our cookie preferences panel or by enabling the "Do Not Track" setting in your browser. While there is no universal standard for "Do Not Track" signals, Momentum honors Do Not Track requests by disabling analytics cookies for visitors who have this setting enabled.
