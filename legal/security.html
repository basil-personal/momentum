Security at Momentum

Privacy and security are not features — they are the foundation of everything we build.

Our Security Philosophy

Momentum captures some of the most sensitive data imaginable: your conversations. We believe that the only way to earn the trust required for this responsibility is to build security into every layer of our architecture, from the hardware itself to the cloud infrastructure that supports it. Our approach is simple — process data on-device whenever possible, encrypt everything, and minimize the data we collect and retain. Security is not an afterthought or an add-on at Momentum; it is a core engineering principle that guides every product decision we make.

Architecture Overview: On-Device Processing

Unlike most AI transcription services that send your audio to the cloud for processing, Momentum performs the majority of its transcription and summarization directly on the wearable device itself. The Momentum device contains a dedicated neural processing unit (NPU) that runs our proprietary AI models locally, without any network connection required. This means your conversations are transcribed on-device in real time, raw audio never leaves the device by default, transcriptions are stored locally in an encrypted partition, and the device can function fully offline with no internet connection.

This on-device-first architecture provides a fundamentally stronger privacy and security posture than cloud-dependent alternatives. Even in the event of a network breach, your audio data remains protected because it was never transmitted in the first place.

Data Flow

Understanding how your data moves through the Momentum ecosystem is essential to understanding our security model. Here is an overview of the data flow:

  1. Audio Capture: The Momentum device's microphone array captures ambient audio. This audio is immediately fed into the on-device AI processor and is never written to persistent storage in raw form.
  2. On-Device Transcription: The neural processing unit transcribes audio in real time. The resulting text is stored in an AES-256 encrypted partition on the device's internal storage.
  3. Local Access: You can access your transcriptions directly via the Momentum companion app over a local Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) connection. This connection is authenticated and encrypted using the BLE Secure Connections protocol.
  4. Cloud Sync (Optional): If you enable cloud sync, transcription text (never raw audio) is transmitted to Momentum's cloud infrastructure over a TLS 1.3 encrypted connection. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest in our cloud storage.
  5. Cloud-Enhanced Transcription (Optional): If you opt in, anonymized audio segments may be sent to our cloud servers for enhanced processing. These segments are encrypted in transit, processed in an isolated compute environment, and permanently deleted after processing is complete — typically within seconds.
  6. Deletion: When you delete a transcription, it is removed from the device, the companion app, and (if synced) our cloud servers. Cloud deletions propagate to all backup systems within 90 days.

Encryption

Encryption at Rest

All data stored by Momentum — whether on the wearable device, in our mobile app, or in our cloud infrastructure — is encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption, the same standard used by governments and financial institutions worldwide. On the Momentum device, transcription data is stored in a hardware-encrypted partition using a device-unique key derived from a secure element. In our cloud infrastructure, data is encrypted using AWS Key Management Service (KMS) with customer-managed keys, providing an additional layer of control and auditability.

Encryption in Transit

All data transmitted between the Momentum device, companion app, and cloud services is encrypted using TLS 1.3, the latest and most secure version of the Transport Layer Security protocol. We enforce TLS 1.3 exclusively and do not support older protocol versions. Our TLS configuration uses only strong cipher suites, implements certificate pinning in our mobile applications, supports HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) with a minimum one-year max-age, and undergoes regular third-party assessment and monitoring.

End-to-End Encryption

For users who require the highest level of security, Momentum supports optional end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for cloud-synced data. When E2EE is enabled, your transcription data is encrypted on your device using a key derived from your account passphrase before it is transmitted to our servers. This means that Momentum cannot read your transcription data on our servers — only you and the devices you have authorized can decrypt it. Note that enabling E2EE disables certain cloud features that require server-side access to your data, such as web dashboard search and third-party integrations.

Certifications and Compliance

Momentum's cloud infrastructure and operational practices have been independently audited and certified under SOC 2 Type II standards. This certification verifies that our systems and processes meet rigorous criteria for security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. Access Controls

Momentum employs strict access controls to protect your data at every level:

  • Employee Access: Access to customer data is restricted on a strict need-to-know basis. Only a small number of senior engineering and support personnel have access to production systems, and all access is logged and monitored. Employees undergo background checks and complete regular security training.
  • Authentication: Momentum accounts support multi-factor authentication (MFA) via authenticator apps (TOTP) and hardware security keys (WebAuthn/FIDO2). We strongly recommend enabling MFA on all accounts. Enterprise customers can enforce MFA requirements for their organization.
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): For team and enterprise accounts, administrators can define roles and permissions to control which team members can access, share, or manage transcription data. Audit logs track all access and permission changes.
  • Session Management: All sessions are time-limited and can be remotely revoked. You can view and manage active sessions from your account settings. Suspicious login attempts trigger automatic notifications and may require additional verification.
  • Infrastructure Access: Access to Momentum's production infrastructure requires VPN access, SSH key authentication, and hardware MFA tokens. All infrastructure access is logged to an immutable audit trail.

Infrastructure Security

Momentum's cloud services are hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS) in SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certified data centers. Our infrastructure security measures include network segmentation and isolation using Virtual Private Clouds (VPCs), web application firewalls (WAF) and distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) protection, automated vulnerability scanning and patch management, container-based deployments with minimal attack surface, immutable infrastructure with automated provisioning and configuration management, and continuous monitoring with real-time alerting for security events. We conduct regular disaster recovery testing and maintain geographically redundant backups to ensure data availability and resilience.

Application Security

Security is embedded throughout our software development lifecycle:

  • Secure Development: All code undergoes peer review with a security focus. We use static analysis (SAST), dynamic analysis (DAST), and software composition analysis (SCA) tools to identify vulnerabilities before code reaches production.
  • Dependency Management: We continuously monitor our software dependencies for known vulnerabilities and apply patches promptly. Our CI/CD pipeline automatically blocks deployments that include dependencies with known critical vulnerabilities.
  • Penetration Testing: We engage independent, reputable third-party security firms to conduct penetration testing of our applications and infrastructure at least annually. Findings are remediated promptly and verified through retesting.
  • Incident Response: We maintain a comprehensive incident response plan that is tested and updated regularly. In the event of a security incident, we will notify affected users promptly and in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

Vulnerability Disclosure Program

Momentum maintains a responsible vulnerability disclosure program. We welcome reports from security researchers and the broader community about potential security vulnerabilities in our products and services. If you discover a security vulnerability, please report it to us at security@momentum.so. Please include a detailed description of the vulnerability, steps to reproduce the issue, the potential impact, and your contact information. We ask that you give us a reasonable period of time (at least 90 days) to investigate and address the vulnerability before making any public disclosure. We will not take legal action against individuals who discover and report vulnerabilities in good faith and in compliance with this policy.

Bug Bounty Program

Momentum operates a bug bounty program to reward security researchers who identify and responsibly disclose qualifying vulnerabilities. Bounty amounts are determined based on the severity and impact of the vulnerability:

  • Critical (remote code execution, authentication bypass, data breach): $2,000 – $10,000
  • High (privilege escalation, significant data exposure, stored XSS): $500 – $2,000
  • Medium (reflected XSS, CSRF, information disclosure): $100 – $500
  • Low (minor information disclosure, best practice violations): Recognition on our security hall of fame

To be eligible for a bounty, the vulnerability must be previously unknown to Momentum, must be reported before any public disclosure, and must not involve social engineering, physical attacks, or denial-of-service attacks against our production systems. All bounty submissions should be sent to security@momentum.so with the subject line "Bug Bounty Submission." We aim to acknowledge receipt within 2 business days and provide an initial assessment within 10 business days.
