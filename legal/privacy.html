Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 1, 2026

1. Introduction

Momentum Technologies, Inc. ("Momentum," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our wearable hardware device, companion mobile application, web dashboard, and related services (collectively, the "Services"). By accessing or using our Services, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this Privacy Policy, please do not access or use our Services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account Information

When you create a Momentum account, we collect information that you provide directly to us, including your name, email address, password (stored in hashed form), billing address, payment method details (processed and stored by our third-party payment processor, Stripe), and any optional profile information you choose to provide such as your job title, organization name, or profile photo. If you sign up using a third-party authentication provider such as Google or Apple, we receive your name and email address from that provider in accordance with their privacy policies.

2.2 Device and Hardware Data

When you use the Momentum wearable device, we may collect certain device-related information, including your device model and serial number, firmware version, battery status, Bluetooth connection data, Wi-Fi network identifiers (but not passwords), and general device health and diagnostic telemetry. This information helps us ensure your device is functioning properly and allows us to deliver firmware updates and troubleshoot issues.

2.3 Transcription and Audio Data

The Momentum device captures ambient audio for the purpose of generating meeting transcriptions and contextual notes. Audio processing occurs primarily on-device using our proprietary AI engine. By default, raw audio is never transmitted to our servers and is discarded immediately after on-device transcription. Transcription text generated by the device may be synced to your Momentum account for storage, search, and retrieval, depending on your settings. If you opt in to our cloud-enhanced transcription feature, anonymized audio segments may be transmitted to our secure servers for improved accuracy, but these segments are processed in real time and are not stored beyond the processing session unless you explicitly enable transcription improvement contributions.

2.4 Usage Data

We automatically collect certain information about how you interact with our Services, including the features you use, the actions you take within the app, the frequency and duration of your sessions, page views and navigation paths on our website, crash reports and performance logs, and the date and time of your interactions. This usage data is collected through analytics tools and is used to improve the functionality and user experience of our Services.

2.5 Location Data

We do not collect precise geolocation data. The Momentum device does not include GPS hardware. We may infer your approximate location based on your IP address for the purposes of providing region-appropriate services, complying with local laws, and detecting potentially unauthorized account access.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide, operate, maintain, and improve our Services, including delivering transcriptions, summaries, and action items from your meetings.
  • To process transactions and send you related information, including purchase confirmations, invoices, and subscription renewal notices.
  • To create and manage your account, authenticate your identity, and provide customer support.
  • To send you technical notices, updates, security alerts, firmware updates, and administrative messages.
  • To respond to your comments, questions, and requests, and to provide customer service and technical support.
  • To communicate with you about products, services, offers, promotions, and events offered by Momentum, and to provide news and information we think will be of interest to you (you may opt out of marketing communications at any time).
  • To monitor and analyze trends, usage, and activities in connection with our Services for research, development, and improvement purposes.
  • To detect, investigate, and prevent fraudulent transactions, abuse, and other illegal activities, and to protect the rights, property, and safety of Momentum and others.
  • To personalize and improve your experience with our Services, including by providing content and features that match your profile and interests.
  • To comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

4. Data Processing: On-Device vs. Cloud

Momentum is designed with a privacy-first architecture. The majority of audio processing and transcription occurs directly on your Momentum wearable device using our on-device AI models. This means your conversations are processed locally, without being sent to external servers, providing a fundamentally higher level of privacy than cloud-only alternatives.

Certain optional features require cloud processing to function. These include cloud-enhanced transcription for improved accuracy in challenging audio environments, cross-device sync of transcriptions and notes, collaborative features such as shared meeting notes and team workspaces, long-term search and archival of transcription history, and integrations with third-party services such as calendar, CRM, and project management tools. When cloud processing is used, your data is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.3 and encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption. You may disable cloud features at any time from your account settings, in which case all processing will remain entirely on-device.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Momentum does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes. We do not share your transcription content with advertisers or data brokers. We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

5.1 Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party companies and individuals to perform services on our behalf, such as payment processing (Stripe), cloud infrastructure (Amazon Web Services), email delivery (SendGrid), analytics (Mixpanel), and customer support tools (Intercom). These service providers have access to your personal information only to perform specific tasks on our behalf and are contractually obligated not to disclose or use it for any other purpose. All service providers are subject to data processing agreements that require them to maintain the confidentiality and security of your data.

5.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in the good-faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, court order, or legal process served on Momentum; to protect and defend the rights or property of Momentum; to prevent or investigate possible wrongdoing in connection with the Services; to protect the personal safety of users of the Services or the public; or to protect against legal liability.

5.3 Business Transfers

If Momentum is involved in a merger, acquisition, asset sale, bankruptcy, or reorganization, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will provide notice before your personal information is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy. In such an event, we will require the acquiring entity to honor the commitments made in this Privacy Policy or obtain your renewed consent.

5.4 With Your Consent

We may share your information with third parties when you have given us your explicit consent to do so, such as when you choose to connect a third-party integration or share transcription content with colleagues.

6. Data Retention

Momentum retains your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with our Services. You can configure your data retention preferences in your account settings. The default retention period for transcription data is one (1) year from the date of creation, after which transcriptions are automatically deleted unless you have chosen a different retention period. Available retention options include 30 days, 90 days, 6 months, 1 year, 2 years, or indefinite retention.

Account information is retained for the duration of your account's existence and for up to 30 days after account deletion to allow for account recovery. Payment records and transaction history are retained for seven (7) years to comply with tax and financial reporting obligations. Aggregated, anonymized usage data that cannot be used to identify you may be retained indefinitely for analytics and product improvement purposes.

When data is deleted, it is removed from our active systems within 30 days and from our backup systems within 90 days.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information. These rights may include:

7.1 Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you. You can access much of your information directly through your account settings and the Momentum app. For a comprehensive data export, you may submit a request through your account settings or by contacting us at privacy@momentum.so.

7.2 Correction

You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you. You can update your account information directly through the Momentum app or web dashboard at any time.

7.3 Deletion

You have the right to request the deletion of your personal information. You may delete individual transcriptions at any time from within the app. To delete your entire account and all associated data, you may do so from your account settings or by contacting us. Please note that we may retain certain information as required by law or for legitimate business purposes, such as fraud prevention and financial record-keeping.

7.4 Data Portability

You have the right to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. Momentum supports data export in JSON, CSV, and plain text formats. You may initiate a data export from your account settings at any time.

7.5 Opt-Out

Please note that even if you opt out of marketing communications, we may still send you transactional and administrative messages related to your account and use of the Services.

In certain circumstances, you may have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information. When processing is restricted, we will continue to store your information but will not process it further without your consent, except for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.
