Do Not Sell My Personal Information

Last updated: March 1, 2026

1. Momentum Does Not Sell Your Personal Information

Momentum Technologies, Inc. ("Momentum") does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for monetary or other valuable consideration. We have never sold personal information, and we have no plans to do so. This has been our practice since the company's founding, and it is a core principle of how we operate.

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), "selling" personal information broadly includes making personal information available to third parties for monetary or other valuable consideration. We confirm that Momentum does not engage in the "sale" of personal information as defined by the CCPA/CPRA. We also do not "share" personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising purposes, as defined by the CPRA.

While we do not sell or share your personal information, we are committed to providing California residents and all users with full transparency and control over their data. This page explains your rights and how to exercise them.

2. Your Rights Under the CCPA/CPRA

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the CCPA/CPRA:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request that we disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources from which we collected your personal information, the business or commercial purpose for collecting your personal information, and the categories of third parties with whom we share your personal information.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete any personal information we have collected about you, subject to certain exceptions permitted by law (such as information needed to complete a transaction, detect security incidents, or comply with legal obligations).
  • Right to Correct: You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.
  • Right to Opt Out of Sale/Sharing: You have the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information. Since Momentum does not sell or share personal information, this right is already honored by default.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Information: You have the right to limit the use and disclosure of your sensitive personal information to purposes necessary to perform the services you have requested.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: You have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your privacy rights. We will not deny you services, charge you different prices, or provide a different level of quality because you exercised your privacy rights.

4. How to Submit a Request

You may submit a request to exercise any of the rights described above using the form below, or by contacting us directly. We accept requests through the following channels:

Online Request Form

Use the form below to submit a privacy rights request. We will respond to your request within 45 calendar days of receipt. If we need additional time (up to an additional 45 days), we will notify you of the reason and the expected response date.

Please use the email address associated with your Momentum account, if applicable.

Email

You may also submit a request by emailing us at privacy@momentum.so with the subject line "CCPA Privacy Rights Request." Please include your full name, the email address associated with your Momentum account, and the specific right you wish to exercise.

5. Verification Process

To protect your privacy and security, we are required to verify your identity before fulfilling your request. The verification process depends on the type of request:

  • Requests to Know (categories): We will verify your identity to a reasonable degree of certainty by matching at least two data points you provide with data points we maintain.
  • Requests to Know (specific pieces) and Requests to Delete: We will verify your identity to a reasonably high degree of certainty by matching at least three data points you provide with data points we maintain, and we may require you to provide a signed declaration under penalty of perjury that you are the consumer whose personal information is the subject of the request.

If you submit a request through an authorized agent, we may require the agent to provide proof of written authorization from you and we may also require you to verify your identity directly with us.

6. Response Timeline

We will acknowledge receipt of your request within 10 business days. We will respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 calendar days of receiving your request. If we require more time (up to an additional 45 calendar days), we will inform you of the reason and extension period in writing. If we are unable to verify your identity, we will notify you and explain why we cannot fulfill your request. Any disclosures we provide will cover the 12-month period preceding our receipt of your verifiable consumer request.

7. Authorized Agents

You may designate an authorized agent to submit a privacy rights request on your behalf. To do so, you must provide the authorized agent with written permission signed by you, and the authorized agent must submit proof of this authorization along with the request. We may also require you to verify your own identity directly with us and to directly confirm with us that you provided the authorized agent permission to submit the request. Authorized agent requests that do not include proper verification will not be processed.

8. Annual Disclosure Metrics

In accordance with the CCPA/CPRA, Momentum will compile and publish annual metrics regarding the number of requests to know, delete, correct, and opt out received in the preceding calendar year, along with the median response time and the number of requests that were denied. These metrics will be published on this page by July 1 of each year for the preceding calendar year.
